Beautiful Beaches

Bonaire, Sint Eustatius & Saba boast some of the most stunning beaches in the Caribbean.

Sorobon Beach in Bonaire is famous for its white sand and clear waters, making it a windsurfing paradise.

On Saba, Cove Bay offers a peaceful retreat with breathtaking coastal views.

History & Culture

These islands have a rich history dating back to indigenous settlements before European colonization.

Bonaire was a Dutch colony, and its culture is a mix of African, European, and Caribbean influences.

Sint Eustatius was once a major trading hub in the 18th century, known as "The Golden Rock."

Saba, with its rugged terrain, remained isolated and developed unique traditions over the centuries.

Delicious Cuisine

The local cuisine is a delightful fusion of Dutch and Caribbean flavors.

Popular dishes include Keshi Yena (stuffed cheese), fresh seafood, and goat stew.

Street vendors offer delicious pastechi (savory pastries), while beachfront restaurants serve grilled lobster and conch fritters.

Economic Highlights

The economy of these islands is primarily driven by tourism, fishing, and salt production.

Bonaire is known for its eco-tourism and diving industry, while Sint Eustatius has a growing oil storage sector.

Saba, being the smallest, focuses on education and healthcare as key economic drivers.

Sports & Activities

Adventure seekers can enjoy world-class diving and snorkeling in Bonaire, with over 80 dive sites teeming with marine life.

Saba is a haven for hikers, featuring the famous Mount Scenery trail, leading to the highest point in the Netherlands.

Windsurfing, sailing, and deep-sea fishing are also popular activities.
